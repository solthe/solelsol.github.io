    Background.

    Our emotions are easily reflected onto the words we write and speak. Detecting the anomaly in these words could mean a significant emotional change in the society.

    Can the words used in the internet reflect the negative emotional change in the society?

    Reading "Detection of the CHange in Blogger Sentiment using Multivariate Control Charts" (2013) by Jeounghoon Moon and Sungim lee, our team member Soojin thought that the current prevalent emotions in the society can also be detected via the 'words of the people'.
    We sought to collect and analyze the blog articles of Naver, the biggest search engine in Korea, to figure out if the anomaly in their sentiments matched the societal issues and negative emotions at the time.
    Can we detect societal problems by detecting
    the sentiment anomaly from the internet such as the twitter posts?

    Data Collection

    Using the Selenium crawler, we have collected 1,000 blog posts a day, saving all data into crawling_naver_api.ipynb: savefile('keyword');
    In total, we have crawled 357 days worth of blog posts and times it by 1,000 we have collected 357,000 blog data with the keyword = daily. This was to collect the daily posts like diaries, ones that people write about their typical days and daily life and not others.

    Sentiment Analysis

    Using the KNU Korean sentiment dictionary, we managed to dissect the words into negative and postivie. This dictionary uses a Bi-LSTM model , that detects the polarity with 89.45% accuracy.
    We then scored each of the words to very negtaive, negative, neutral, positive and very positive, and categorized them accordingly. Then, we plotted the scores of 357 blog posts to a scatter graph.

    Result Analysis

    We were able to detect a correlation between COVID-19 case reports and the low sentiment scores accordingly.


    Blue: COVID-19 case reports Turquoise: Sentiment score
    It turns out the anomaly in graph represented the societal emotions according to the sentiment score dispersions, after analyzing and crawling the relevant news articles at the time.
    The negative trend in sentiment anomaly signified the negative societal atmosphere at the time, while positive trend signified there was a good turn in the news or in the society. See below.

    Conclusion

    It as a whole showcased that analyzing and simply detecting the sentiment anomaly, whether positive or negative, can predict the kind of societial and envrironmental news of the day or in the near future. We can further do the research on other media platforms, create sentiment dictionary or predict the upcoming social sentiments.
